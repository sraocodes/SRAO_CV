Vehicular Robot Prototype for Collision Avoidance

B.Eng in Electronics and Communication, 2010

As a capstone for my Bachelor of Engineering degree, I undertook a project that involved the development of a prototype for a vehicular robot. The primary objective was to ensure collision avoidance using advanced electronic components and programming methodologies.

Key Achievements and Tasks:

  • Development and integration of the 8051 microcontroller, which was programmed using Assembly language and HDL Verilog.
  • Hands-on design and assembly of the circuit, which required meticulous soldering techniques to ensure the system’s durability and functionality.
  • Active collaboration with my project partner, Praveen, fostering an environment of teamwork.
  • Successful demonstration of the prototype in real-world scenarios.
Skills:
8051 microcontroller Assembly language Circuit design
Simulation of Plasma Thruster Dynamics

Research Assistant, 2011-2012

I undertook a significant role in simulating particle-in-cell dynamics of a plasma thruster during my tenure as a research assistant at the University of Alabama. Under the guidance of Professor Nagendra Singh, I delved deep into several technical aspects of plasma dynamics and thruster simulation. The following highlights capture the essence of my work and contributions:

  • Key Achievements and Tasks:
    • Developed and optimized FORTRAN codes tailored for the simulation.
    • Implemented advanced algorithms to solve Poisson’s equations.
    • Innovatively tracked particle movements in self-generated electrostatic fields.
    • Conducted a comprehensive study on the formation of electric double layers in helicon plasma devices.
    • Leveraged the capabilities of the Alabama Supercomputer for high-performance computing tasks, ensuring accurate and timely simulation results.
    • Designed and executed MATLAB scripts, enhancing data visualization and processing of extensive simulation datasets.
  • Publication: Our research findings were prominently acknowledged and were published in the prestigious Physics of Plasmas journal in 2012.
Skills:
Fortran High-Performance Computing MATLAB Scripting
Analysis of Alfvén Wave Structures via Electromagnetic Particle-in-Cell Simulation

Research Assistant, 2011-2012

During this period, I was deeply involved in a project centered on particle-in-cell simulation with a focus on electromagnetic coding. The core aim was to decipher the Alfvén wave structures, a pivotal phenomenon with applications in understanding solar dynamics.

Through the use of an impulse function, we successfully generated an Alfvén wave in our simulation, subsequently analyzing the subsequent wave patterns produced within the plasma. Notably, the observed spectra generated mirrored experimental values, highlighting the accuracy and efficacy of our code.

Key Achievements and Tasks:

  • Mastered the MPI in FORTRAN, crucial for executing parallel jobs on the Alabama Supercomputer.
  • Developed an electromagnetic code tailored for the project’s requirements.
  • Achieved spectral results that were in alignment with experimental data.
  • Enhanced understanding of Alfvén waves, furthering insights into solar dynamics.
Skills:
Fortran Parallel Computing (MPI) Scientific Computation
